MIT Design For America

The Problem

How can we empower college students with the skills, confidence, and creativity to make an impact?

The Solution

Build a community of passionate changemakers to help each other build impactful projects.

MIT Design for America is a club dedicated to making a social impact in the community. We connect students with real people to solve real problems.

As DFA, we run hackshops and project teams throughout the year. Project teams work on yearlong projects tackling problems in our local community such as homelessness and poor education. Hackshops are monthly skill-building workshops open to all students and built to help them acquire new skills and learn about how to apply those to human-centered design.

We strive to create a community of passionate changemakers who support each other in making a difference.

My Role

I helped to found DFA in 2012 and served as president for one year and a project team mentor for one year. I led the publicizing efforts to attract over 300 students to DFA over the years and grow our influence across campus. I also initiated the hackshop, project team, and design critique programs and composed a complete design guide to help guide the project teams through the year.
