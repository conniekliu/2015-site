FingerReader

The Problem

How can we help the blind have more independence when reading on the go?

The Solution

A camera mounted ring to empower blind users to interact with the text around them.

FingerReader is a text-reading assistive device for the blind. It is designed to be portable and assist blind people in reading text in everyday situations.

With just the point of a finger, visually impaired users can have menus, books, forms, and more read to them in real time. The device gives the user the freedom to read full paragraphs, scan through lines to identify content of interest, and navigate through multiple pages with ease.

The device provides vibration and tonal feedbacks to help guide the user through text. A vibration signal is triggered at the beginning and end of each line, and tones help alert the user whenever he or she strays off of the line.

Lessons Learned

Used dual material 3D printing to create the ring and Arduino for smart vibration feedbacks and LEDs.

Worked on a tight timeline to rapidly prototype various design iterations based on user feedback.

Made the vibration feedback more distinct and improved wearer comfort by adding rubber material to the ring design.

Created novel solutions for distinguishing intentional writing movements from tremor movements using digital motion processing.
